Geographical position

Are you looking for a job in a specific geographic location that will ease your commute to work or allow you to pick up your kids after school? Should your place of work be on a direct bus route? Put it on your resume.

Wage rate

Are you looking for a specific pay rate? This may mean that you need to choose a job with tougher conditions in order to meet your income requirements. Or perhaps you're willing to charge less money if the job involves being creative.

Working hours

Are you looking for work during certain hours? Do you think best at night? Do you have to be home at certain times due to family obligations? Are you ready to take shifts? These items also need to be known to the employer.

your latest work

Is there any specific reason why you left your past or previous job? Does it affect the next job you want? Write it down in the "About Me" section.


The "About Me" section matters because it's where you talk about your preferences. This allows you to find the best match for the vacancy and bypass many competitors even at the stage of reviewing the resume.
